Frequently Asked Questions

Questions & Answers

If you don't see your question here, drop us a note — we respond to every inquiry within one business day.

What kind of banks do you serve?
Genesis is purpose-built for community banks, credit unions, regional lenders, and specialty finance institutions. If you're between roughly $250M and $20B in assets, we're squarely in your range — but we've worked outside that band too. The platform scales down without losing features.
How is Genesis different from legacy cores?
Three big differences: (1) real-time event-driven architecture instead of nightly batch, (2) open APIs across every module rather than walled gardens, and (3) consolidated pricing aligned with deposit volume rather than per-seat or per-module gouging. Our technology lineage is Tier-1 — we then re-tuned it for community-bank operating models.
How long does a typical implementation take?
Median engagement for a full core migration is roughly 14 weeks across four phases — discovery & design, build & integrate, migrate & cutover, stabilize & expand. Module-only adoption (e.g., just origination or just payments) can be considerably shorter. We never recommend flag-day cutovers.
Can we keep our existing systems and integrate Genesis modules?
Yes. Genesis is modular and API-first. Many banks start with one or two modules — origination, payments, or compliance — alongside their incumbent core, and migrate further as they're ready.
What does your security and compliance posture look like?
SOC 2 controls, encryption in transit and at rest, role-based access with full audit logging, continuous compliance monitoring, and dedicated security review for each customer deployment. Our security documentation is available under NDA on request.
What support do you provide after go-live?
24/7 monitoring across every Genesis instance, a dedicated response team with documented incident playbooks, a 15-minute SLA on P1 issues, a named technical account manager for your bank, and quarterly business reviews. Patching and maintenance run on zero-downtime windows.
How does the Design Partner Program work?
Limited enrollment — typically eight banks per cohort, 12-month engagements. Partners sit on the Genesis Design Committee and help shape the product roadmap, receive customized features without funding the R&D, and get cost-effective upgrades and operations. See our Partnership page for details.
What's your pricing model?
Predictable pricing aligned with deposit volume rather than seat count, with no "enterprise tier" gatekeeping — every bank gets the full feature set. Implementation is a fixed engagement; ongoing platform fees scale with your business. We're happy to share specifics under NDA during a scoping conversation.
Where can I see the platform in action?
Book a working session through our contact page. We'll demo live, map your current stack against the Genesis modules, and walk through what a phased adoption would look like.
